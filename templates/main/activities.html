Activities

Despite the name, the ANU Mountaineering Club is involved in a broad range of outdoor activities including bushwalking, snowshoeing, canyoning, climbing, mountain biking, kayaking, rogaining, cross country skiing and mountaineering. The club runs weekend trips as well as regular weekly events catering for a wide range of fitness and experience levels. The activities pages provide more details about the different activities. Trips can be viewed or submitted through the trip calendar page.

The ANU Mountaineering Club runs a huge variety of activities throughout the year. Whether you’re looking to push your limits or simply enjoy the outdoors, there’s an adventure for you:

  • Climbing & bouldering: Weekly sessions at local climbing gyms, single‑pitch outings and weekend trips to crags around Canberra and the Blue Mountains. Instruction is provided for beginners.
  • Bushwalking & hiking: Day hikes, overnight walks and multi‑day expeditions. Discover alpine meadows, temperate rainforests and rugged ridgelines in the ACT and NSW.
  • Canyoning: Experience the thrill of water‑filled slot canyons. Trips range from easy swims and scrambles to technical descents requiring abseiling and rope skills.
  • Skiing & snowboarding: Back‑country touring, downhill days at resorts and introduction to cross‑country skiing. Trips cater to beginners as well as seasoned ski tourers.
  • Kayaking & canoeing: Flatwater paddling on Lake Burley Griffin, white‑water trips on the Murrumbidgee and multi‑day canoe journeys. Learn paddling techniques and rescue skills.
  • Caving: Go underground in the limestone systems of Yarrangobilly and Wee Jasper. Trips cover horizontal caves as well as vertical pitches using single rope techniques.
  • Social events: BBQs, trivia nights, gear maintenance evenings and the annual “Cocktails on the Castle”. Socials are a great way to meet other members and share stories.

All trips are led by volunteer leaders. If you’re new to an activity, mention this in your sign‑up and the leader will ensure the trip is appropriate for your level. As you gain experience, you can train to become a leader yourself.

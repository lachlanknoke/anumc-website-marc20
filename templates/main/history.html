History and Hall of Fame

The ANU Mountaineering Club has a proud history stretching back more than half a century. Founded by a handful of adventurous students keen to explore the hills around Canberra, the club quickly grew into one of the most active university outdoor clubs in Australia.

Over the decades, members have pioneered first ascents, discovered hidden caves, skied remote backcountry routes and introduced hundreds of novices to the joy of the outdoors. The club has also produced world‑class climbers, mountaineers and expedition leaders who have gone on to climb in the Himalaya, Alps, Andes and Alaska.

Highlights from our history include:

  • The first recorded ascent of Booroomba Rocks by club members in the 1960s.
  • Regular expeditions to Tasmania’s remote Southwest wilderness.
  • Introducing caving to the ACT region and mapping systems in Yarrangobilly and Wee Jasper.
  • The development of an inclusive trip leader training program that has become the benchmark for university outdoor clubs.
  • The annual publication of “Epic”, our magazine showcasing trip reports, essays and photography from members past and present.

Our Hall of Fame celebrates members who have made outstanding contributions to the club’s spirit and success. It recognises leaders, volunteers and athletes who embody our values of safety, inclusivity, respect for the environment and a passion for adventure. Nominees are announced at the AGM each year.

If you have stories, photos or memorabilia from the club’s past, please get in touch—we’d love to feature them here and preserve them for future generations of ANUMC members.

Club Ethics

The ANUMC is committed to creating a safe, inclusive and environmentally responsible outdoor community. We expect all members to uphold the following principles on every trip and at every event:

  • Respect for the environment: Practise Leave No Trace. Stay on established tracks where possible, pack out all rubbish, minimise campfire impacts and protect wildlife and vegetation.
  • Safety first: Follow the instructions of your trip leader, carry appropriate gear, know your limits and speak up if you feel unsafe. Leaders are trained to manage risk but safety is a shared responsibility.
  • Inclusivity: We welcome people of all backgrounds, identities and experience levels. Treat others with respect, support newcomers and contribute to a positive, friendly culture free from harassment and discrimination.
  • Responsibility: Arrive on time, come prepared and commit to the trips you sign up for. If you need to cancel, notify the leader as early as possible so someone on the waitlist can take your place.
  • Alcohol and drugs: Consumption of alcohol or drugs is not permitted during trips or in the field. Social events may involve alcohol in moderation; always drink responsibly and look out for your mates.
  • Develop your skills: Take opportunities to learn new skills and, when ready, give back by volunteering, leading trips or helping at the gear store.
